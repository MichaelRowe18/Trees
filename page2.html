How to Resolve Tree Damage

Trees can be harmed by many environmental factors like storms, drought, or flooding. This page offers simple steps to help you take care of damaged trees.     Fixing Damage from Bad Weather

    After a storm, remove any broken or hanging branches to prevent injury or disease. Use clean tools to make smooth cuts. If the tree is leaning, support it with a stake. For long-term care, plant trees that are more resistant to strong wind or snow.

    Fixing Damage from Drought

    Water your trees deeply once or twice a week during dry seasons. Add mulch around the base of the tree (but not touching the trunk) to help keep moisture in the soil. Avoid cutting the tree during a drought to reduce extra stress.

    Fixing Damage from Flooding

    If a tree has been flooded, try to drain the extra water if possible. Aerate the soil around the tree to help roots breathe and recover. In the future, plant trees in well-draining soil or raise the planting area to avoid future flooding.
